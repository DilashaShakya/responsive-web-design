Empowering Women, Reshaping Futures.

Anuradha Koirala is a Nepali social activist and the founder of Maiti Nepal, a non-profit organization dedicated to combating human trafficking, especially of women and children. Born in 1949 in Kathmandu, Nepal, she worked as a teacher for over 20 years before devoting her life to social work.

Important dates

  • Founded Maiti Nepal in 1993 with a small shelter and big mission: to rescue, rehabilitate, and reintegrate survivors of sex trafficking.
  • Over 50,000 women and children have been helped by Maiti Nepal through rescue operations, border monitoring, rehabilitation centers, and educational outreach.
  • She pioneered a network of surveillance at Nepal-India border checkpoints,where staff intervene and investigate suspicious cases of trafficking.
